### Summary

Keshav Singh and Rachik Sharma discussed the MOSIP platform's support for birth and death registration flows, including the child new birth registration where parental national IDs are authenticated and CRVS acts as the source of truth, and the death registration process where MOSIP flags the UIN as "deceased equals to true" without deactivation. The meeting also addressed handling infant biometrics, the use of the \`create packet\` API for various flows including demographic data updates, and the importance of parental/informant authentication via e-signet for both birth and death registrations.

Rachik Sharma detailed the handling of rare scenarios, such as ID deactivation for fraudulent child birth registrations, which requires manual verification and submission of a new request for reactivation, and reversing the deceased flag for adults, which also involves manual verification. Technical details discussed included the packet processing workflow, the lack of failure notifications to CRVS, the rationale against allowing CRVS to perform demographic updates for individuals with biometric linkages, and the removal of support for offline integration.

The UIN remains active to support services tied to the national ID, but relying parties receive the deceased flag in user info ([00:03:23](?tab=t.631g42v2km7d#heading=h.jj99b7opqntq)).
